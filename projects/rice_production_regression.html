Linear Regression Analysis of Rice Production in Indonesia 2023

This project applies Linear Regression to analyze and predict rice production across Indonesian provinces using harvested area data published by Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS).

Python · Pandas · Scikit-learn · Matplotlib · Google Colab

Problem & Motivation

Rice is a strategic agricultural commodity in Indonesia. Understanding how harvested area influences rice production is crucial for agricultural planning, food security assessment, and evidence-based policy evaluation at the provincial level.

Dataset Overview

  • Source: Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS)
  • Year: 2023
  • Level: Province
  • Variables: Harvested Area (ha), Rice Production (tons)

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

The scatter plot below visualizes the relationship between harvested area and rice production across Indonesian provinces. Each point represents one province.

A clear positive linear trend is observed, indicating that provinces with larger harvested areas consistently produce more rice. The absence of strong nonlinear patterns or extreme outliers suggests that a linear model is suitable for this dataset.

Modeling Approach

A simple linear regression model was trained to quantify the relationship between harvested area (independent variable) and rice production (dependent variable).

The regression line closely follows the distribution of the data points, indicating that the model effectively captures the underlying trend. The relatively tight spread of points around the line suggests a stable and consistent relationship across provinces.

Model Evaluation

The plot below compares actual rice production values with model predictions. Most points lie very close to the diagonal reference line, indicating high predictive accuracy across provinces with varying production scales.

An R² score of 0.995 implies that nearly all variability in rice production can be explained by harvested area alone, demonstrating the strong explanatory power of the model.

The residual plot below shows no clear systematic pattern, indicating that model errors are randomly distributed. This supports the assumptions of linear regression and suggests that the model does not exhibit significant bias across different production levels.

Key Insights

  • Harvested area is a dominant factor influencing rice production at the provincial level.
  • The relationship between area and production is strongly linear and consistent.
  • Simple linear regression performs exceptionally well for aggregated agricultural data.

Limitations & Future Work

This model relies on a single explanatory variable. Future improvements may include yield per hectare, climate variables, irrigation coverage, or multi-year time-series data to capture temporal dynamics.
